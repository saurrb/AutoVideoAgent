# Page 4 V2 Panning Story-Strip Rules

Use this rule set when Page 4 generates landscape images for panning reels.

## Core Idea

The final reel merges all generated landscape triptych images side by side, then shows a moving 9:16 crop across the full merged strip. Therefore Grok must not create single centered poster images. Each image should be a horizontal relationship story/thought strip with three useful sections.

## Image Composition Rules

- Generate one landscape 16:9 image, not portrait.
- Compose each image as exactly three sections: left, center, and right.
- Each section must be visually different and include red glow/red emotional light.
- The left third, center third, and right third must each work as a standalone 9:16 vertical frame.
- Each third should contain its own readable mini-beat:
  - Left third: setup, emotional cue, or first problem.
  - Center third: tension, turning point, or symbolic conflict.
  - Right third: consequence, repair, or next emotional beat.
- Do not place all important characters or action in the center.
- Spread important silhouettes, props, light cues, and emotional symbols across the full width.
- Use foreground, midground, and background details so the pan feels like discovering a story.
- Keep the lower caption zone calmer than the rest of the frame.

## V2 Art Direction

- Simple premium relationship cartoon scene.
- Moody blue-black apartment/living-room world with crisp outlines, soft shadows, and warm rim light.
- Strong red glow/red emotional line as the recurring brand motif.
- Mature, serious, premium, not cute.
- Recurring characters only: Maya and Leo. They are the actual people experiencing the relationship tension, not observers.
- Maya: smooth white circular face, tiny black dot eyes, worried brows, short black bob, dark charcoal sweater, muted olive pants.
- Leo: smooth white circular face, tiny black dot eyes, worried brows, short neat black hair, dark navy button-up, charcoal pants.

## Negative Rules

- No text, letters, captions, signs, speech bubbles, watermark, or logo inside the generated image.
- Not cute.
- Not 3D.
- Not photorealistic.
- Do not create a centered poster composition.
- Do not add analyst, narrator, therapist, observer, clipboard, watermark, or logo inside the generated image.
- Do not leave large empty wall space across the pan.

## Prompt Fragment

```text
This image will become part of a merged horizontal story strip shown through a moving 9:16 vertical crop, so compose it as a three-section relationship story/thought strip, not as one centered poster.
The left third, center third, and right third must each work as a standalone vertical 9:16 frame with its own readable mini-beat.
Create a clear left-to-right emotional progression across the same continuous environment: setup on the left, tension or turning point in the center, consequence or repair beat on the right.
Avoid placing all important characters or action in the center. Spread important silhouettes, props, light cues, and emotional symbols across the full width.
Use foreground, midground, and background details so the pan feels like discovering a story, not sliding across empty wall.
```
